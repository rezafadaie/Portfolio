Business intelligence and data analysis for movie theater project

June 12, 2020
  1. The goal of the project

The goal of this project is to establish a unique movie theater in Milan catering to international students by exclusively screening films in their original language. By analyzing the questionnaire already provided, research questions encompass exploring consumers’ cinema habits, assessing attitudes towards dubbed films, investigating potential demand for an original language theater among international students, and determining strategies to specify offerings that satisfy customer preferences. Additionally, the research aims to examine effective marketing approaches for promotion and engagement, informed by insights derived from the analyzed questionnaire data.

  1. Sample description

The survey conducted for this project reveals certain limitations, notably a biased participant pool consisting exclusively of university students in Milan pursuing Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees. The constrained age range is a direct result of this demographic focus, albeit with efforts made to ensure a balanced gender distribution among participants.

It is acknowledged that the surveyed population, despite the intention to capture international perspectives, is predominantly Italianized. This poses a challenge given the objective of establishing a non-Italian original movies theater in Italy.

To contextualize the financial landscape, the data is oriented towards the student perspective. On average, a resident in Milan expends 880€, whereas students exhibit a substantially lower expenditure, approximately half of this average. These observations highlight the characteristics of the surveyed population and underscore the importance of expanding outreach to diversify participant demographics for a more comprehensive analysis.

  1. Top factors influencing cinema-goers

Initially, the investigation focused on discerning the primary considerations guiding our audience in selecting a movie theater. A preliminary analysis indicates that the paramount factors influencing their choice are:

  • Choice of Films Shown (11%): The variety and selection of films available.

  • Price of Ticket (10%): The cost associated with attending the movie.

  • Time Schedule (Availability by person) (10%): The convenience of showtimes aligning with individual schedules.

The discerning nature of our well-educated population underscores a heightened emphasis on the quality of the movie experience. Despite this, the busy nature of the audience renders both the time schedule and ticket price significant concerns, reflecting a nuanced balance between a desire for cinematic excellence and practical considerations of time and expense.

  1. Original view cinema attributes

Italian native speakers exhibit a preference for Italian movies, mirroring the inclination of English speakers toward films in their native language. In contrast, speakers of Spanish, German, and French, distinct from the former groups, express a notable interest in the concept of an original movie theater, suggesting a potential receptivity to non-native language films. This insight underscores the diverse linguistic preferences within the target audience, indicating an opportunity to cater to varied language backgrounds through the proposed original movie theater concept.

  1. Behavioral segmentation

Conducting behavioral segmentation commenced with a factor analysis, where commonalities and variance proportion explanations were scrutinized, revealing an approximate 70% variance distribution across four factors. Subsequent to a factor rotation process, the identified factors were designated as Trend, Native, Original, and Authentic.

For a clearer visualization of cluster distribution, a determination was made regarding the optimal number of clusters (k). Utilizing Ward’s method as the clustering technique and Euclidean distance as the interval measure in SPSS, a discernment emerged that four clusters provided an optimal representation. These clusters are characterized as follows: Educated Originality Seekers, Marginals, Dubbers, and Trend Followers.

  1. Conclusion

In conclusion, recognizing the limitations of our survey, which focused on students from three major universities in the Milan area with restricted diversity, the strategic consideration of establishing an original movie theater emerges as a viable approach. This geographic concentration allows for tailored marketing activities, particularly emphasizing the preferences of “Marginals” and “Original Content Seekers.”

Expanding beyond Italian films, the inclusion of French, Spanish, and German movies aligns with the preferences of approximately 75% of the surveyed population, presenting a lucrative opportunity for a profitable business, especially given the consistent movie-going habit observed among the majority of respondents.

The envisioned business objective revolves around establishing an Original Content Movie Theater in Milan, strategically located near universities, with a focus on French and Spanish content. The envisioned ambiance combines luxury with affordability, specifically catering to individuals passionate about intriguing cinematic experiences. This comprehensive approach aligns with our findings, ensuring a targeted and appealing offering for the discerning audience.
