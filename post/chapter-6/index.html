The cross-industry role of data science in the Italian Start-ups landscape: focus on Fintech, Fashion, Food, and Marketing

January 20, 2021
  1. The goal of the Project

The goal of this research project is to investigate the pivotal role of data analysis within top Italian startups across various industries, including fashion, fintech, food, and marketing, in the year 2020. This research seeks to elucidate the innovation and competitive edge that each of these startups brings to their respective sectors by addressing the following research questions:

  • To discern how startups can harness and capitalize on (big) data and data analysis techniques to their advantage, thereby enhancing their operations and competitiveness.

  • To ascertain the presence and significance of pure data science companies in Italy’s startup landscape, and their contribution to the overall entrepreneurial ecosystem.

  • To identify and analyze the innovative strategies and distinctive factors that set apart different categories of data-driven startups operating within these diverse industries.

Through rigorous analysis and exploration, this research aims to provide valuable insights into the dynamic relationship between data analysis, innovation, and startup success in the Italian entrepreneurial landscape.

  1. Dataset

The companies within the dataset have been carefully curated based on three key criteria: the amount of capital they have successfully raised, the speed at which they are actively expanding their workforce, and their substantial contributions to the development and nurturing of the broader startup ecosystems in which they operate. This meticulous selection process ensures that the dataset comprises companies that have not only demonstrated notable financial prowess but have also displayed a significant and influential presence within their respective entrepreneurial environments.

  1. Fintech

Despite being among the most heavily regulated sectors, the FinTech industry consistently grapples with cybersecurity challenges, as evidenced by recurring headlines featuring financial product-related cybercrimes. The frequency of such security risks underscores the necessity for governments to proactively devise novel regulations and legislation at the national level. Our research indicates a predominant focus among Italian FinTech startups on the payment and investment domains, leveraging the introduction of new applications to enhance customer services. Notably, a substantial majority of these startups have emerged within the last decade, with a particular concentration between 2 and 10 years ago, indicative of a heightened proclivity for launching ventures in this industry during the past ten years.

Looking ahead, the trajectory of the FinTech and banking sectors hinges on the adept implementation of agile technologies and the fortification of data security policies. In this digital era, meeting the evolving demands of technology-savvy customers and remaining at the forefront of technological progress are imperative for sustaining competitiveness. As the landscape continues to evolve, the strategic integration of innovative technologies and robust security measures will undoubtedly shape the future landscape of the FinTech and banking industries.

  1. Fashion

Over the past five years, a limited number of higher education fashion programs have initiated certification courses focusing on the optimization of data analytics and AI-based technologies. These courses aim to cultivate strategic thinking among fashion students regarding data-driven decision-making and to integrate data analytics into design and merchandising strategies. Recognizing the imperative for students to possess skills in utilizing consumer data for decision-making, there is a growing need to incorporate data analytics programs into existing fashion education.

The surge in demand for professionals equipped with skills in fashion data analytics underscores the industry’s increasing recognition of the transformative power of data analytics. Implementing data analytics into current fashion programs requires collaborative efforts with technology partners offering AI-powered data analytics services tailored to the needs of fashion brands and retailers. Fashion educators play a pivotal role in not only instilling essential fashion fundamentals but also fostering technological literacy and adaptability among students, given the industry’s escalating digitization.

An examination of four prominent fashion startups in Italy reveals a notable focus, with three specializing in apparel, and Artemest distinguishing itself by exclusively offering luxury furniture. Notably, PrivateGriffe stands out by providing a platform for customers to both buy and sell products, differing from other startups where customers can solely place orders without the option to sell their own items.

In conclusion, the evolving landscape of AI learning algorithms, enriched by richer data and sophisticated technology, is anticipated to deliver more inclusive and personalized experiences for consumers. However, the efficacy of machine learning models relies on diverse datasets provided by stakeholders in the fashion and science industries. It is essential for these datasets to be inclusive, ensuring utility across diverse demographics and industries.

Despite the prowess of data science, it remains incapable of singularly dictating trends and generating new ideas. In the dynamic realm of fashion, the emergence of original styles and rapid shifts in preferences are not uncommon. While data science and analytics optimize human choices and facilitate predictive technology, the intrinsic “art” of the fashion process lies within the realm of human creativity, asserting the ultimate influence on the final decisions in the industry.

  1. Food

In light of the foregoing, it is evident that Big Data solutions offer a plethora of applications across various sectors within the food industry. The integration of such technology provides a foundation for the formulation of highly effective strategies, particularly in leveraging predictive analytics capabilities driven by artificial intelligence. While numerous companies provide food tech solutions, the challenge lies in finding a provider that precisely aligns with one’s specific requirements.

This study focuses on a comprehensive examination of seven prominent Italian startups within the food industry. Notably, the majority of these startups are headquartered in Milan, with additional establishments in Firenze and Gavorrano. Primarily oriented towards agriculture and forestry.

Our research reveals a consistent trend among the top Italian startups in the food industry, with a predominant focus on agriculture and a tendency towards maintaining a small to medium-sized workforce. The founding dates of these startups fall within a range of 4 to 11 years ago. Employing data analysis techniques, these startups glean valuable insights into customer behavior, underscoring the transformative role of data analytics in the food industry.

Against the backdrop of a growing global population, climate change, and land desertification, the food industry faces multifaceted challenges. To address these challenges efficiently and innovatively, technology capable of collecting and interpreting data meaningfully becomes imperative. The application of data science and analytics empowers organizations to collect, analyze, and identify patterns and trends, thereby facilitating the development of creative solutions to industry challenges. Consequently, data science emerges as a pivotal tool not only for problem-solving but also for ushering in positive developments within the food and beverage sector.

  1. Marketing

Our investigation into the top Italian startups within the marketing industry reveals a notable trend in their average valuation, ranging between 5 and 35 million euros. The majority of these startups were founded approximately six to seven years ago, with the exception of Legami Srl, established in 2003. Demonstrating a commonality, these startups harness big data and analytics to gain profound insights into customer behavior.

The transformative impact of big data and analytics extends beyond the marketing sector, reshaping the landscape of numerous industries. Technological advancements have ushered in a digital era, enabling businesses to track the digital activities of millions of individuals through diverse techniques, offering an intimate perspective on consumer behavior and purchasing habits. The profound significance of data collection is evident in its role in propelling industries forward and ensuring their alignment with contemporary trends.

Within the marketing domain, the utilization of big data holds immense potential, facilitating informed decision-making for professionals in the field. Real-time generation and collection of big data, operating 24/7, provide valuable insights into consumer activities, including purchases, downloads, and online interactions. The integration of online and offline data enables a more comprehensive understanding of consumer preferences and buying tendencies.

The ubiquity of consumer social interactions online allows the marketing industry to gauge the success of various marketing efforts, enabling swift adjustments to strategies based on real-time feedback. Big data and analytics, thus, become instrumental in refining marketing plans and optimizing their effectiveness.

Furthermore, the rise of big data and analytics in the digital age highlights the profound consequences of internet usage. For new products entering the market, the availability of extensive data simplifies the task of finding their target audience. The intricate process of reaching consumers, identifying preferences, and avoiding less receptive segments is streamlined through the use of data and analytics. Established brands also benefit, not only by maintaining relevance with existing demographics but also by uncovering new consumer segments previously unknown.

In essence, big data provides a competitive advantage to both emerging and established products, shaping a landscape where strategic decision-making and consumer engagement are enhanced through comprehensive data-driven insights.

  1. Conclusion

In the course of our investigation, we meticulously scrutinized 29 prominent startups spanning four distinct industries in Italy. Notably, all of these startups share a founding period falling within the expansive timeframe of 2000 to 2020, with a predominant concentration in Milan.

Among the industries under consideration, Fintech emerges with the highest average valuation, commanding an impressive 48.36 million euros. In contrast, the Fashion industry exhibits the lowest average value among the studied sectors, standing at 15.88 million euros. This divergence in average valuations underscores the varying financial landscapes and market dynamics within these industries, reflective of their respective growth trajectories and market positioning.

This comprehensive analysis provides valuable insights into the temporal and geographic distribution of startup establishments, as well as a comparative understanding of their financial valuations across distinct industry sectors in Italy.
