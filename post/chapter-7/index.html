Flight Delay and Cancelation Analysis for Airlines

September 18, 2024

1. The goal of the Project

The goal of this project was to analyze and visualize trends in flight delays and cancellations across multiple airlines from 2009 to 2018. The analysis aimed to provide insights into delay rates, cancellation reasons, and the financial impact of delays, helping airlines make data-driven decisions to improve operations and minimize losses.

2. Dashboards

  • Overview Dashboard:
    This dashboard provides a high-level summary of total flights, cancellations, delays, and the cost associated with flight delays. It allows users to quickly understand the scale of operations and identify key areas of concern.

  • Cancellation Dashboard:
    The cancellation dashboard focuses on analyzing the reasons for flight cancellations and visualizes the number of cancellations over time. Users can filter the data by date to track trends and uncover patterns in cancellations, helping airlines understand the most frequent causes of flight disruptions.

  • Delay Dashboard:
    This dashboard digs deeper into flight delays, showing reasons for delays and the overall average delay per flight by date. It includes a comparison feature, allowing users to contrast the average delay per flight for each airline against the industry average, providing insights into airline-specific performance.

  • Information Dashboard:
    The information dashboard offers detailed metadata and descriptions of the dataset, helping users better understand the context, sources, and structure of the data used in the analysis.

On the first page, users can easily navigate to any of these dashboards using buttons labeled Overview, Cancellation, and Delay. This intuitive navigation feature allows users to access the relevant information quickly and efficiently.

Users can also utilize the menu bar to navigate to the relevant dashboards for easy access to the information they need.

3. Dataset

The dataset contains detailed flight information from 2009 to 2018, covering multiple airlines. Key columns include:

  • FL_DATE: Flight date
  • OP_CARRIER: Airline identifier
  • DEP_DELAY/ARR_DELAY: Departure and arrival delays (minutes)
  • CANCELLED: Indicates if a flight was canceled
  • CANCELLATION_CODE: Reason for cancellation (Airline, Weather, NAS, Security)
  • DISTANCE: Distance between airports
  • CARRIER_DELAY/WEATHER_DELAY/NAS_DELAY: Delay reasons

3.1.Calculated Columns

I created additional columns to enhance analysis:

Arrival/Departure Delay Category: Short or long delay Distance Category: Short, medium, or long distance Cost of Delay: Calculated based on delay time and distance

4. Overview Dashboard

In this dashboard, I analyzed key metrics such as total flights, total cancellations, average departure delay, average arrival delay, and the total cost of delays. A bar chart visualizes the number of flights per airline, while another chart shows flight activity by date. Users can filter the data by specific dates and airlines for more detailed insights. Each card includes dynamic up or down arrows, indicating changes compared to the previous year.

Additionally, I used a DAX measure to highlight the maximum and minimum number of flights by date, dynamically changing the colors to green for the max value and red for the min value, allowing users to easily spot peak and low flight activity.

5. Cancelation Dashboard

In this dashboard, I analyzed the reasons for flight cancellations, showing that weather-related cancellations were the highest and security-related cancellations were the lowest. The dashboard includes visualizations of cancellations by date, with the highest values highlighted in green and the lowest in red for easy identification. Users can filter the data by year and different airlines to gain more specific insights.

6. Delay Dashboard

This dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of flight delays. It highlights that late aircraft delays are the most frequent, while security-related delays are the least common. The dashboard includes analyses of the total cost associated with long and short delays, the number of short and long delays, and distance categories.

A key feature is the line chart, which displays the overall average delay per flight over time. Users can select different airlines to add specific lines for each airline in the chart, allowing for direct comparison between individual airline delays and the overall average delay per flight.

The line chart on the Delay Dashboard reveals that, overall, airline AA experiences a higher average delay per flight compared to the industry average. Notably, in 2017, airline AA’s average delay per flight was lower than the overall average, indicating a temporary improvement in delay performance during that year.

7. Conclusion

This project provided valuable insights into flight delays and cancellations across multiple airlines from 2009 to 2018. By developing detailed dashboards, the analysis enabled a thorough understanding of key metrics such as total flights, cancellations, delays, and their financial impacts.

Benefits of this Project:

  • Enhanced Operational Insight: Airlines can identify trends and patterns in delays and cancellations, helping to pinpoint operational inefficiencies and areas for improvement.

  • Data-Driven Decision-Making: The dashboards facilitate informed decision-making by presenting complex data in an accessible and actionable format, including comparisons between different airlines and time periods.

  • Cost Management: By analyzing the cost of delays and categorizing them, airlines can better manage and potentially reduce costs associated with delays.

  • Trend Identification: The visualizations make it easy to track performance over time and identify significant trends, allowing airlines to address issues proactively.

Overall, this project equips airlines with the tools needed to optimize their operations, reduce delays, and enhance overall efficiency through comprehensive and interactive data analysis.
