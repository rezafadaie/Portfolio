Predictive Analysis of Term Deposit Subscription in Direct Marketing Campaigns

March 13, 2021
  1. Abstract:

This project revolves around the analysis and prediction of term deposit subscription in the context of direct marketing campaigns conducted by a Portuguese banking institution. The dataset used for this analysis comprises two subsets, namely “bank-full.csv” and “bank.csv,” containing information on client demographics, contact details, and historical campaign data. The primary objective is to develop a predictive model to determine whether a client will subscribe to a term deposit, indicated by the binary variable “y” (yes or no).

  1. Introduction:

Direct marketing campaigns play a pivotal role in the banking sector for promoting financial products and services. In this study, I investigate the factors influencing the success of marketing campaigns aimed at promoting term deposits. The dataset spans from May 2008 to November 2010 and contains a diverse set of features related to clients, their interactions with the bank, and the outcomes of previous marketing efforts.

  1. Data Description:

The dataset comprises the following input variables:

  • Age (numeric)
  • Job: Type of job (categorical)
  • Marital: Marital status (categorical)
  • Education: Education level (categorical)
  • Default: Presence of credit in default (binary)
  • Balance: Average yearly balance in euros (numeric)
  • Housing: Presence of a housing loan (binary)
  • Loan: Presence of a personal loan (binary)
  • Contact: Communication type for the last contact (categorical)
  • Day: Last contact day of the month (numeric)
  • Month: Last contact month of the year (categorical)
  • Duration: Last contact duration in seconds (numeric)
  • Campaign: Number of contacts performed during the current campaign (numeric)
  • Pdays: Number of days since the client’s last contact from a previous campaign (numeric, -1 indicating no previous contact)
  • Previous: Number of contacts performed before the current campaign for the same client (numeric)
  • Poutcome: Outcome of the previous marketing campaign (categorical)

The output variable to be predicted is:

  • Y: Subscription to a term deposit (binary: “yes” or “no”)

  1. Project Goal: The primary goal of this project is to develop a predictive model that can accurately classify whether a client will subscribe to a term deposit based on the provided dataset. The insights gained from this analysis can aid the Portuguese banking institution in optimizing its marketing strategies, identifying potential clients, and improving campaign effectiveness.

  2. Methodology: The project will involve the following steps:

  • Data Preprocessing: Cleaning, handling missing values, encoding categorical variables, and scaling numeric features.
  • Exploratory Data Analysis: Analyzing the dataset to gain insights into feature distributions, correlations, and identifying patterns.
  • Feature Engineering: Creating relevant features or transforming existing ones to improve model performance.
  • Model Selection: Evaluating and selecting suitable machine learning algorithms for classification.
  • Model Training: Training the selected model(s) on the dataset.
  • Model Evaluation: Assessing model performance using appropriate metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score.
  • Model Optimization: Fine-tuning hyperparameters to enhance predictive accuracy.
  • Interpretability: Understanding the key features contributing to term deposit subscriptions.
  • Deployment: Implementing the predictive model for practical use in future marketing campaigns.
  1. Conclusion:

By the end of this project, I aim to provide the banking institution with a powerful tool for predicting term deposit subscriptions, enabling them to make data-driven decisions that can significantly impact the success of their direct marketing campaigns. It’s worth noting that Ann had the highest performance in implementing the predictive model, which can serve as a benchmark for future improvements and insights in the banking institution’s marketing strategies.
